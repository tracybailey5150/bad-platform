# BAD Business Plan

## 1. Executive Summary

BAD (Business Automation & Development) is a business automation, custom software, AI integration, and consulting company focused on helping small and midsize businesses replace manual workflows, disconnected tools, and spreadsheet-driven operations with purpose-built systems.

BAD's core offer is not generic software development. It is the design, integration, and delivery of:
- Custom business apps
- Workflow automation systems
- Dashboards and reporting platforms
- Scheduling and intake systems
- AI-assisted workflow tools
- Consulting-led system design and implementation

## 2. Problem

Many small and midsize businesses still run critical operations through email, spreadsheets, disconnected SaaS tools, manual follow-up, weak handoff processes, and fragmented visibility across teams. This creates lost leads, delayed responses, poor reporting, inconsistent customer experience, and operational bottlenecks. The need is not "more software." The need is better systems built around how the business actually works.

## 3. Solution

BAD solves this by combining consulting + integration + custom build:
- **Lead Response Systems** — capture, qualify, route, and follow up with leads
- **Workflow & Operations Apps** — replace manual status tracking and spreadsheet-heavy processes
- **Scheduling + Intake Systems** — simplify booking, intake, reminders, and admin control
- **Dashboards & Reporting Platforms** — give owners and managers real visibility
- **AI Integration** — summarization, routing suggestions, intake cleanup, draft responses, workflow recommendations
- **Consulting-led implementation** — so clients get the right system, not just code

## 4. Target Market

Primary targets:
- Service businesses
- Contractors and field teams
- Appointment-based businesses
- Professional service firms
- Operations-heavy SMBs

Ideal customer profile: ~5-100 employees, already using multiple tools, feeling pain from manual work and weak visibility, willing to pay to improve speed, coordination, and reporting.

## 5. Business Model

Hybrid services-to-SaaS:

| Revenue Stream | Pricing |
|---|---|
| Discovery / Consulting | $500 - $1,500 |
| Custom Implementation | $3,000 - $12,000+ |
| Monthly Platform/Support | $199 - $999+/mo |
| Productized Modules | Setup + subscription |
| Future SaaS | As patterns emerge |

## 6. Go-to-Market Strategy

- **LinkedIn** — primary B2B trust and authority channel
- **YouTube** — proof channel for demos and workflow teardowns
- **Website** — badsaas.app with clear CTA: "Book a Workflow Review"
- **Warm outreach and referrals** — target operators feeling workflow pain
- **Productized sample builds** — clickable demos, before/after comparisons

## 7. Product Roadmap

**Phase 1:** Launch brand, publish solutions, create sample builds, offer workflow reviews, close first clients
**Phase 2:** Standardize modules (intake, workflow, dashboard, scheduling, notifications, AI assist)
**Phase 3:** Package into semi-productized offers, add subscriptions, develop SaaS modules

## 8. Technology Stack

- Next.js + React + Tailwind (frontend)
- Supabase (auth, database, storage, realtime)
- Trigger.dev (background workflows)
- Resend (transactional email)
- Stripe (billing)
- Integrations: CRM, calendar, SMS providers

## 9. Closing Positioning

BAD helps businesses replace manual work, disconnected tools, and operational friction with custom software, workflow automation, AI integration, and consulting-led systems that improve visibility, speed, and execution.
